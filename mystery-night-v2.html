What Kind of Game Is This?

The Format

Think Together, Solve Together

Each team gets a case: a crime, a set of suspects, and facts. Clues unlock every few minutes — automatically. Your team discusses, debates, and eliminates suspects. At the end, you vote on your culprit. That's it.

What Makes It Fun

The argument IS the game

The cases are designed so multiple suspects seem guilty at first. Every clue flips the table. Half the fun is confidently accusing someone… and being completely wrong three clues later.

Game Flow

  • 0:00 — Scene is revealedGM reads the case aloud. Team reads the suspect list together. Everyone can see everything on one shared screen.
  • 0:03 — First clues unlockTwo starter clues are visible immediately. Team begins discussing. Point a finger too early — you'll regret it.
  • 0:10 — Clue Wave 2 unlocksNew evidence appears. Old theories crumble. New suspects rise. Chaos.
  • 0:20 — Clue Wave 3 unlocksThe critical clue. One clear pattern emerges — if your team has been paying attention.
  • 0:30 — Final VoteGM calls time. Team nominates one culprit. Reveal happens. Scores awarded.

Rules of the Game

The Golden Rule

Everything you need is on screen. No notes required.

You will not need to write anything down. Clues stay visible once unlocked. Suspect details are always there. Your only job is to talk to your team and form a theory.

Do This

✅ Read the scene together
✅ Discuss each suspect out loud
✅ Challenge each other's theories
✅ Wait for new clues before deciding
✅ Enjoy being wrong

Don't Do This

❌ Take notes — clues don't disappear
❌ Lock in your answer too early
❌ Google the answer (yes, someone always tries)
❌ Talk over each other in the last 2 minutes
❌ Accuse someone just because you dislike them

Discussion Sparks

If your team goes quiet, throw one of these into the group:

"Who actually had access to do this — physically?"

"Who had the most to gain if this crime succeeded?"

"Who is behaving strangely and hasn't been questioned yet?"

"Which suspect's alibi is suspiciously perfect?"

"Let's eliminate the impossible — who definitely couldn't have done it?"

Family Friend, 38
Old friend of Rohit's. Invited last minute. Runs an antiques trading business. Was "exploring the farmhouse" late at night. Nobody knows him well.

Evidence

Clue 01 — Visible Now
Kamla's bedroom door was found slightly ajar in the morning. She always locks it. Her key was inside the room. The lock shows no signs of tampering.

Clue 04 — Wave 2

Case II — The Last Reservation

35:00
Time Remaining
Investigation Progress0 / 6 clues unlocked
📋 The Scene
Acclaimed chef Vikram Sood is found unconscious inside his own restaurant, "Saffron," at 11:30 PM — locked inside the kitchen from the inside. The back exit is bolted with a chain only accessible from within. His sous chef Preet found him after hearing a crash. Vikram was alive but had been drugged — traces of a sedative were found in his evening chai. He had no memory of the evening. A valuable signed recipe book — the original manuscript of his bestselling cookbook — was missing from his office safe. Five people had access to the restaurant that evening.
💬 Discuss First

The kitchen was locked from inside — meaning either Vikram locked himself in, OR someone locked it and left through another way. What ways could someone exit a restaurant without using the main or back doors?

The Suspects

Preet Kaur
Sous Chef, 29
Vikram's most trusted assistant. Has been with him 5 years. Was the one who "found" him. Claims she was in the dining area the whole evening doing inventory.
Tarun Mehta
Restaurant Manager, 36
Manages front-of-house. Had access to the safe code for supplier payments. Left early — says his mother called him at 9 PM. Returned when Preet called him.
Lata Bose
Food Critic, 47
Was a guest for a private tasting session that evening. Known to have publicly called Vikram's book "overrated." Left at 10 PM — or so she says.
Aryan Sood
Vikram's Nephew, 24
Was helping with the event. Has a gambling debt he's been hiding from the family. Has been seen browsing rare book auction sites on the office computer.
Meena Pillai
Cleaning Staff, 51
Has worked at Saffron for 8 years. Knows every corner of the restaurant. Was scheduled to clean that night. Claims she left at 9:30 PM without entering the kitchen.

Evidence

Clue 01 — Visible Now
The sedative found in Vikram's chai is a common over-the-counter antihistamine in high doses — not a prescription drug. Anyone could have obtained it.
Clue 02 — Visible Now
The restaurant has a staff bathroom with a window large enough for a person to climb through. It connects to an alley behind the building — separate from the back door.
Clue 03 — Wave 2
Aryan's browser history shows visits to a rare books auction site. The signed manuscript was listed anonymously two days after the incident — later traced to a seller in the city.
Clue 04 — Wave 2
Lata Bose's jacket was found in the staff bathroom — she claims she forgot it. But the jacket was hanging on the inside of the bathroom window latch.
Clue 05 — Wave 3
Meena the cleaner told police she saw Lata Bose still inside the restaurant at 10:40 PM — not 10 PM as Lata claimed. Lata was near the office corridor, not the exit.
Clue 06 — The Critical Clue
The safe was opened using Tarun's code — but Tarun's phone location data shows he was at a petrol station 6 km away at 9:45 PM. He could not have opened the safe after 9 PM. Someone else used his code.
🔥 Hot Discussion Starter

"Focus on the escape route — whoever did this had to leave without being seen. Which suspect had both the knowledge and the opportunity to do that?"

Game Master Guide

🔒 Host Eyes Only

This page contains full solutions, red herrings explained, and tips for managing both groups. Don't share this screen!

Case I — Full Solution

The Culprit

Sunita Malhotra — The Daughter-in-Law

Sunita knew about the loan Rohit owed to Kabir. She overheard a tense conversation between them days before the wedding. Fearing Kabir would embarrass the family or seize assets, she took the necklace herself — planning to sell it quietly and repay the debt. She used Renu's spare key (which she'd borrowed "to organise gifts") to enter Kamla's room between 10:45 and 11 PM, which is why she appeared in the background of a photo at 10:50 — heading back from the hallway. Her alibi of a 9:45 PM bedtime was entirely fabricated.

Motive

Fear of debt exposure + protecting family reputation. Not greed — panic.

Method

Borrowed spare key from Renu (under false pretext). Entered during the party when noise covered movement. Room was dark — Kamla was asleep.

Red Herrings — Explain to group post-game

Rohit — the debt looks suspicious but he had no need to steal from his own mother when he was already negotiating an extension.

Kabir — the antiques dealer looks perfect for it, but he was never near Kamla's room and had no motive to steal something he'd have to secretly sell.

Dev — his 20-minute disappearance was genuinely innocent; he went to check on his grandmother (a tender moment ruined by suspicion).

Case II — Full Solution

The Culprit

Lata Bose — The Food Critic

Lata had been publicly humiliated by Vikram, who called out her "inaccurate" reviews in an interview. She attended the private tasting specifically to steal the original manuscript — knowing it would fetch a high price and, more importantly, ruin his legacy. She drugged his chai during the tasting, waited for him to fall unconscious, opened the safe using a code she'd shoulder-surfed from Tarun during a supplier visit two weeks prior, took the manuscript, locked the kitchen from inside (leaving Vikram there), and exited through the staff bathroom window — leaving her jacket behind by mistake. Aryan's browsing history was coincidental and unrelated to this crime.

Motive

Personal vendetta + financial gain from selling the rare manuscript.

Method

Sedative in chai → safe opened with stolen code → kitchen locked from inside → bathroom window exit.

Red Herrings — Explain to group post-game

Aryan — the gambling debt + auction browsing makes him look perfect. He was browsing for legitimate reasons (checking values of his uncle's collection as a surprise gift for a family event).

Tarun — his safe code was used, which looks damning. But his verified location alibi clears him completely.

Preet — "the person who found the body" is always suspicious. But her inventory logs (checked later) confirmed her presence in the dining area.

If Teams Finish Too Fast

Throw in a curveball question: "Wait — but how did she know the safe code? Walk me through that logic step by step." Asking teams to reconstruct the full timeline of events, not just name the culprit, adds 10 minutes of rich discussion naturally.

If Teams Are Stuck

Give a nudge without giving it away. For Case I: "Think about who lied about where they were and what that lie hides." For Case II: "Focus on the escape route — who would know about that bathroom window, and who left evidence there?"
